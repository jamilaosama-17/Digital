Student Reviews

Straight from Our Students

★★★★★

From the moment I started this major, I realized how practical it is. Most of the work we do is hands-on, which makes the learning experience much more engaging. One thing I didn't expect at all was that we would study programming and learn how to code — that honestly came as a surprise, but turned out to be a very valuable part of the major. I found my internship opportunity through the university's job fair. We worked mainly on social media content — going out, shooting videos, and preparing them for publishing. It was a really enjoyable experience. What makes this major stand out is that it is not purely theoretical. You are not completely shocked when you enter the job market — you already have some level of experience. The university is improving every year and bringing more opportunities, and that is clearly getting better over time.

Leena

★★★★★

My internship experience was genuinely eye-opening. I joined the marketing team and was involved in real campaigns from start to finish — not just observing, but actively contributing. It was fast-paced and sometimes overwhelming, but incredibly rewarding. One of the biggest differences I noticed is that in the workplace, you're expected to take initiative, ask the right questions, and deliver results without constant guidance. During my internship, I applied concepts like audience segmentation, the marketing funnel, and consumer behavior to real campaigns — seeing these ideas work in practice made everything we studied feel much more relevant. This experience helped me overcome self-doubt and build real confidence — not just from studying, but from actually delivering results. It showed me how dynamic, strategic, and data-driven Digital Marketing really is.

Mazin

★★★★★

My internship experience felt like being thrown into the deep end — in the best way possible. From the very first week, I was involved in real meetings, contributing ideas, and working on tasks that actually mattered. It wasn't just theoretical learning, it was real responsibility. One of the biggest differences I noticed is that in the real world, 'done and effective' often matters more than 'perfect but late.' I applied concepts like buyer personas in real scenarios, helping build strategies based on actual customer data — that's when everything we studied started to feel truly relevant. Most importantly, it changed how I see Digital Marketing — it's not just about creating content, but about creating impact. Every decision is driven by strategy and results.

Mohamed
